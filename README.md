# StreamFlow Customer Churn Analytics

# Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges faced by subscription-based businesses. Losing customers directly impacts revenue, growth, customer retention, and customer lifetime value (CLV).

This project analyzes customer behavior, subscription patterns, engagement metrics, and customer support interactions to identify:

Why customers churn:
- Which customer segments are most likely to churn
- Revenue lost due to churn
- Customer engagement patterns
- High-value customer segments
- Acquisition channels driving revenue

The project combines SQL, Python (Pandas & NumPy), Excel, and Power BI to perform end-to-end analytics and build an interactive business dashboard.

# Business Problem

StreamFlow, a subscription-based video streaming platform, wants to answer the following business questions:

- What is the overall churn rate?
- Which subscription plans have the highest churn?
- Which customer segments generate the most revenue?
- Which acquisition channels bring valuable customers?
- How does customer engagement impact retention?
- What factors contribute to customer churn?
- What is the financial impact of churn?

# Tools & Technologies
- SQL (Oracle SQL)
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Excel
- Power BI
- Git & GitHub

# Key Insights

- Free Trial customers exhibit the highest churn rate.
- Premium, Family, and Pro subscribers show stronger retention.
- Customers with lower watch hours are more likely to churn.
- Negative support experiences increase churn probability.
- Higher engagement levels are associated with better retention.

# Business Recommendations

- Improve Free Trial conversion rates through targeted retention and onboarding strategies.
- Increase customer engagement by promoting personalized content recommendations.
- Launch proactive retention campaigns for customers showing low activity levels.
- Enhance customer support quality to improve customer satisfaction and reduce churn.
- Focus marketing investments on high-value subscription plans and acquisition channels that generate long-term revenue.
